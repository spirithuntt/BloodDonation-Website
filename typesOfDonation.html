Types of Donation

There are several types of blood donations, each serving a different purpose. Here are the most common types:

Platelet Donation

Platelets are cells in the blood that help with clotting. Platelet donation involves the separation of platelets from the rest of the blood components, and the remaining blood is returned to the donor's body. Platelet donations can take up to two hours to complete.

Plasma Donation

Plasma is the liquid part of blood that carries nutrients, hormones, and proteins. Plasma donation involves the separation of plasma from the rest of the blood components, and the remaining blood is returned to the donor's body. Plasma donations can take up to an hour to complete.

Double Red Cell Donation

This type of donation allows donors to give twice the amount of red blood cells in a single donation. During a double red cell donation, a machine separates red blood cells from the other blood components and returns the other blood components to the donor's body. Double red cell donations take longer than whole blood donations, and donors must meet certain eligibility criteria

Directed Donation

Directed donation allows a donor to give blood for a specific person, such as a family member or friend, who needs a transfusion. Directed donations must be scheduled in advance and the donor must meet certain eligibility criteria

Autologous Donation

Autologous donation involves a donor giving blood for their own use, such as before a surgery. The donated blood is stored and given back to the donor during or after their procedure.

Whole Blood Donation

This is the most common type of blood donation, where a donor gives one pint (about 470 ml) of whole blood. Whole blood contains red blood cells, white blood cells, and plasma.
